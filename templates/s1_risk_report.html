Signal 01 · Financial Track · Positive

Revenue growth of 31% YoY is the primary financial strength signal

GST-declared turnover grew from approximately ₹21.7 Cr (FY24) to ₹28.4 Cr (FY25). For a 6-year-old B2B verification platform, this growth rate is strong and positions the entity in the upper-BB financial scoring zone. Cross-verified with MCA-filed financials — variance under ₹30L, within acceptable threshold.

⚑ Watchpoint — Net margins at 7.2% remain thin relative to revenue growth. If growth decelerates while costs remain fixed, profitability could compress below sustainable levels within 2–3 quarters.
Signal 02 · Behavioural Track · Watchpoint

EPFO headcount fell from 178 to 142 employees during a period of 31% revenue growth

A 20% reduction in PF-registered employees while revenue is growing is an unusual combination for a verification technology company. This may reflect a deliberate shift to contractor-heavy or gig-based staffing. However, without third-party confirmation, it is treated as a conduct risk signal in BehaviourPrint™ and contributes to a lower Conduct & Behaviour dimension score.

⚑ Watchpoint — If headcount continues to fall below 120 employees in the next 6 months, re-run full assessment. Sustained contraction alongside flat revenue would be a material early-stress signal.
Signal 03 · Legal Track · Watchpoint

One active commercial court matter — pre-decree, no injunction

CNR search identified one matter at a Commercial Court (Delhi jurisdiction) at a pre-decree stage. No injunction, attachment, or execution proceedings are visible. Assessed as a B2B contract dispute. Does not meet ZeroPass™ threshold at current stage, but adds approximately 0.6% to Blended PD through the Legal track of Tri-Core™.

⚑ Watchpoint — If this matter progresses to decree, execution, or attachment before next assessment cycle, it must be treated as a ZeroPass™ re-evaluation trigger. Monitor via Service 3 if exposure is active.
Signal 04 · Behavioural Track · Watchpoint

No institutional lenders on charge register — no third-party credit discipline layer

MCA21 charge register shows no charges registered by any bank, NBFC, or financial institution. For a company with ₹28.4 Cr in revenue, this means no external lender has conducted credit appraisal or holds a security interest. While not negative in isolation for an asset-light SaaS business, it removes a validation layer that sellers often rely on as an indirect signal of creditworthiness.

⚑ Watchpoint — For exposures above ₹20L, the absence of institutional lenders should be factored into collateral and advance-payment structuring. Upgrade to Service 2 for a computed safe exposure limit calibrated to this entity's specific financial position.
Signal 05 · Financial Track · Watchpoint

DSO of 54 days — elevated for a SaaS-plus-verification platform

Debtor collection cycle of 54 days is above the expected norm for a SaaS or B2B API-led business, where payment cycles are typically tighter. Combined with thin net margins, an elevated DSO creates a mild working capital sensitivity. This has been factored into the Financial track and contributes a modest downward pressure on the financial dimension score.

⚑ Watchpoint — Extended payment terms should not be offered until DSO shows improvement across two consecutive assessment cycles. Service 2 computes the safe exposure limit and recommended tenor based on this entity's specific CCC profile.
